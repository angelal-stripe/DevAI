Agentic Commerce Protocol (ACP)

How AI agents, businesses, and payment processors transact — including platforms (Connect) and Meta / Facebook.
Sources: agenticcommerce.dev · Stripe newsroom · Stripe docs · Platforms guide

Generic ACP Flow Open Standard
Payment credentials Shared Payment Token (SPT) Product / order data Funds settlement

Buyer

Provides a payment method to the AI agent (e.g. saved card in their ChatGPT or Meta wallet). Never shares raw card numbers — credentials are tokenized.

AI Agent

Discovers products, renders checkout UI, collects buyer selections. Issues a Shared Payment Token (SPT) via Stripe and passes it to the business using ACP. The agent is not the merchant of record.

Stripe

Creates and validates SPTs, processes payments via PaymentIntent, settles funds to the business. First PSP to support ACP. Never exposes raw credentials.

Business (Seller)

Merchant of record. Controls product catalog, pricing, and fulfillment. Receives the SPT, creates a PaymentIntent, and gets paid by Stripe.

Facebook / Meta — Checkout via Ads ACP in Practice
Meta surfaces / interaction Stripe payment rails Funds flow Fulfillment / config

Meta / Facebook's Role

Distribution surface, not merchant of record. Meta shows ads and surfaces a native checkout UI powered by Stripe. It uses the buyer's saved credentials from their Meta wallet to create an SPT. Meta earns revenue from ads, not from the commerce transaction itself.

Stripe's Role

Payment infrastructure. Stripe issues SPTs, processes PaymentIntents, and settles funds to the business. Businesses opt in via a toggle in the Stripe Dashboard and link their Meta ads account — no separate integration needed.

Flow of Funds

Buyer → Stripe → Business. The business is charged Stripe processing fees as usual. Meta does not touch the payment funds. The ACP protocol coordinates the checkout, but all money movement happens on Stripe rails.

Agreements

Business ↔ Stripe (existing processing agreement). Business ↔ Meta (ads account linkage, opt-in to sell via FB). Buyer ↔ Meta (Meta wallet / saved payment credentials). ACP is the open protocol gluing these together.

Agentic Commerce for Platforms (Connect) Stripe Connect
Platform actions Stripe / SPT rails Funds flow Webhooks / hooks Config / fulfillment

Platform (SaaS / Marketplace)

Orchestrator, not the seller. Uploads product catalogs on behalf of connected accounts. Embeds the ConnectAgenticCommerceSettings component so sellers can opt into AI agents. Controls tax, shipping, order approval, and application fees via webhook hooks.

Connected Account (Seller)

Merchant of record. Opts into selling via agents through the embedded component. Stripe creates an Agreement between the seller and each agent. Receives funds directly (minus platform app fee). Handles fulfillment, refunds, and disputes.

Webhook Hooks

Stripe sends two hooks to the platform before completing checkout: customize_checkout (tax rates, shipping options) and finalize_checkout (order approval + application fee). Platform must respond within 4 seconds or the payment is declined.

Charge Types

Platforms choose direct charges (PaymentIntent on connected account) or destination charges with on_behalf_of. In both models the connected account is the seller. Platform monetizes via application_fee_amount returned in the finalize hook.
